Mailroom Kiosk

The public interface I chose is the mailroom kiosk. Below is a visual representation of the interface that students interact with

Part I: Preparation

Below is a sketch of Brown's mail room kiosk and the multiple screens that users will view during interaction.

This interface solves the problem of digitizing the brown mailroom package pickup process. With this interface, students can choose to view the amount of packages they have and pick up their mailroom packages. The interface creates an orderly way for students with an ID to notify mailroom employees that they are present and desire package pickup. Users have to:

  • Approach the kiosk
  • Swipe their card
  • Select to pickup packages or cancel
  • Walk to pickup window

Part II: Recording Observations

Key Observations

  • The average time that users interact with this interface is 10 seconds
  • There is often a line to use this kiosk
  • Although there are id swiping pictures displayed, some people swipe their ID card the incorrect way
  • One user abandoned the kiosk for another when swiping multiple times did not work
  • No users were observed pressing cancel
  • Few users receive the no packages yet screen
  • Users do not seem to read signage around the interface

User Research Questions

  1. How many times a week do you pick up packages?
  2. How does the information about your packages make you feel?
  3. How comfortable do you feel using this kiosk?
  4. Do you come here even when you know you have no packages?
  5. Which direction do you swipe your id? Does it matter?
  6. In what situation would you swipe and then cancel?
  7. How did you learn to use this mail room tablet
  8. Could you teach someone else how to use this?
  9. Are you missing any information?
  10. Do you know how to proceed after you leave the interface?
  11. Has the interface ever shown you something you didn't expect or errored?
  12. What would you do if the interface errored?
  13. Can you try to use this incorrectly?
  14. Are there buttons or functions that you've never pressed? Why?
  15. Are there words on this interface that you don't understand or maybe haven't noticed before?

Summarized Responses

  • All users feel fairly comfortable interacting with this kiosk as they've used it very frequently. Most come with knowledge that a package has arrived while some use the kiosk to check.
  • Users learned to use this kiosk their first year at Brown and recall reading signage and watching others.
  • The three observed users feel as though the information on the interface is thorough and allows them to confidently take next steps in the process. One indicated that she would like to know more about the size of packages
  • All users have experienced errors when swiping their card. Two mention that swiping multiple times often helps the machine recognize their ID while another student prefers to use a new kiosk.
  • Users have all never pressed the x on the first screen and didn't notice there were words written at the bottom of the interface. All of them have never canceled their packages but assume it would take them to the home screen
